The 2019–20 Spartak Moscow season was the twenty-eighth successive season that the club played in the Russian Premier League, the highest tier of association football in Russia.

Season events[edit]

On 29 September, Oleg Kononov resigned as manager with Serhiy Kuznetsov being appointed as Caretaker manager.[1] On 14 October, Domenico Tedesco was appointed as the new head coach on a contract until June 2021.[2]

On 17 March, the Russian Premier League postponed all league fixtures until April 10th due to the COVID-19 pandemic.[3]

On 1 April, the Russian Football Union extended the suspension of football until 31 May.[4]

On 15 May, the Russian Football Union announced that the Russian Premier League season would resume on 21 June.[5]

On 18 May, Spartak Moscow announced that they had exercised their option to purchase Aleksandr Sobolev from Krylia Sovetov.[6]

On 25 June, Spartak Moscow signed a new contracts with Pavel Maslov and Ilya Golosov until the summer of 2024.[7][8]
