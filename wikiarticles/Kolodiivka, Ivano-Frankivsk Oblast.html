Kolodiivka (Ukrainian: Колодіївка, Polish: Kołodziejówka) is a village in the Tysmenytsia Raion (district) of Ivano-Frankivsk Oblast in western Ukraine. The village's population was 571 as of the 2001 Ukrainian census.[2]

The settlement was first founded in the 12th century as Fedkovo (Ukrainian: Федьково); in 1466, the village was renamed to its current name, Kolodiivka.[2]

It is located in the northern portion of the oblast on the Bystrytsia Solotvynska River at an elevation of 301 m (988 ft).[1] The village maintains its own local government as the Kolodiivka Village Council (Ukrainian: Колодіївська сільська рада), which consists of 16 locally elected deputies.[2]
