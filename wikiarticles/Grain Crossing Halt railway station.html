Grain Crossing Halt (TQ 863 753 51°26′45″N 0°40′55″E﻿ / ﻿51.445943°N 0.681966°E﻿ / 51.445943; 0.681966Coordinates: 51°26′45″N 0°40′55″E﻿ / ﻿51.445943°N 0.681966°E﻿ / 51.445943; 0.681966) was a halt on the Hundred of Hoo Railway between Stoke Junction Halt and Port Victoria station in the UK. It was opened on 1 July 1906 and closed to rail traffic (but not passengers) on 11 July 1951. A bus service operated until 3 September 1951, when it was replaced by Grain station. Although officially named Grain Crossing Halt the station nameboard read Grain Halt[1][2]
