Caravan Bridge

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia
Jump to navigation Jump to search

Coordinates: 38°25′19″N 27°09′07″E﻿ / ﻿38.422058°N 27.152059°E﻿ / 38.422058; 27.152059 Caravan Bridge (Turkish: Kemer Köprüsü) is an ancient bridge in the city of İzmir, Turkey, built approximately 850 BC over Meles river, and is one of the oldest man-made structures in continuous use.[1]
