Roman Catholic Diocese of Sarh

From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia
Jump to navigation Jump to search

The Roman Catholic Diocese of Sarh (Latin: Sarhen(sis)) is a diocese in Sarh in the Ecclesiastical province of N'Djamena in Chad.

History[edit]

  • December 22, 1961: Established as Diocese of Fort-Archambault from the Diocese of Fort-Lamy
  • August 22, 1972: Renamed as Diocese of Sarh

Special churches[edit]

The cathedral is Cathédrale Notre Dame de l’Immaculée Conception in Sarh.

Bishops[edit]

Ordinaries, in reverse chronological order[edit]

Bishops of Sarh, below

  • Miguel Ángel Sebastián Martínez MCCJ (10 October 2018 – present)
  • Edmond Jitangar (11 October 1991 – 20 August 2016), appointed Archbishop of N’Djaména
  • Matthias N’Gartéri Mayadi (7 March 1987 – 11 June 1990), appointed Bishop of Moundou
  • Henri Véniat, S.J. (22 August 1972 – 7 March 1987); see below

Bishop of Fort-Archambault, below

  • Henri Véniat, S.J. (22 December 1961 – 22 August 1972); see above

Auxiliary bishop[edit]

  • Matthias N’Gartéri Mayadi (1985-1987), appointed Bishop here
