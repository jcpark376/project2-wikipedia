History[edit]

Hill Spring was founded in 1910 by Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints leader Edward J. Wood.[citation needed]

Demographics[edit]

In the 2016 Census of Population conducted by Statistics Canada, the Village of Hill Spring recorded a population of 162 living in 74 of its 92 total private dwellings, a -12.9% change from its 2011 population of 186. With a land area of 0.96Â km2 (0.37Â sqÂ mi), it had a population density of 168.8/km2 (437.1/sqÂ mi) in 2016.[2]

In the 2011 Census, the Village of Hill Spring had a population of 186 living in 78 of its 83 total dwellings, a -3.1% change from its 2006 population of 192. With a land area of 1.11Â km2 (0.43Â sqÂ mi), it had a population density of 167.6/km2 (434.0/sqÂ mi) in 2011.[3]

Notable people[edit]

Nathan Eldon Tanner, who served in the Alberta Legislature and the First Presidency of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints, lived and taught school in Hill Spring.
