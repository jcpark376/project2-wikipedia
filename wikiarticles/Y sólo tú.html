"Y sólo tú" (Spanish pronunciation: [i ˈsolo ˈtu], "And Only You") is a song recorded by Spanish singer Bacchelli. The song was written by Amado Jaén. It is best known as the Spanish entry at the Eurovision Song Contest 1981, held in Dublin.[1]

Background[edit]

The song is a moderately up-tempo number in which Baccelli sings about his loved one, saying that he is happy only when both are together; "Nada es igual si tú no estás" (translated: "Nothing is the same if you're not here").[2]

Eurovision[edit]

The song was performed tenth on the night, following France's Jean Gabilou with "Humanahum" and preceding the Netherlands' Linda Williams with "Het is een wonder". At the close of voting, it had received 38 points, placing 14th in a field of 20.[3]

It was succeeded as Spanish entry at the 1982 Contest by Lucía with "Él".[4]
